About Arnala Fort

Arnala Fort, built in the 16th century, is a significant historical site located on Arnala Island in Maharashtra. The fort is known for its strategic location and impressive architecture, making it a popular destination for history enthusiasts and tourists alike.

The fort offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea and is surrounded by beautiful landscapes. Visitors can explore the fort's walls, bastions, and the scenic surroundings, making it a perfect spot for a day trip.

The fort is less crowded compared to other tourist spots, providing a peaceful atmosphere for exploration and relaxation.

How to Reach

By Train

The nearest railway station is Virar, which is well-connected to Mumbai. Local trains run frequently.

Distance from station to fort: 10 km

By Road

Take NH48 from Mumbai towards Gujarat. The drive takes about 2 hours. Buses and taxis are also available.

Distance from Mumbai: 100 km

Local Transport

Auto-rickshaws and taxis are available from Virar station to the fort. Renting a bike is also an option for local exploration.

Best Time to Visit

Winter (Oct-Feb)

Ideal season for visiting. The weather is pleasant, making it perfect for exploration and outdoor activities.

Peak Season

December to February sees a surge in visitors. It's advisable to book accommodations in advance during this time.

Tips & Guidelines

Do's

  • Wear comfortable shoes for trekking
  • Carry water and snacks
  • Respect the historical site
  • Take plenty of photos
  • Engage with local guides for insights

Don'ts

  • Don't litter around the fort
  • Avoid climbing on unstable structures
  • Don't disturb wildlife
  • Don't visit during heavy rains
  • Avoid loud noises that may disturb the peace
