About Tarapur Fort

Tarapur Fort, built in the 16th century, is a significant historical site located in the Palghar district of Maharashtra. The fort offers a glimpse into the region's rich history and architecture, making it a popular destination for history enthusiasts and tourists alike.

The fort is strategically located on a hill, providing stunning views of the surrounding landscape and the Arabian Sea. Visitors can explore the fort's ruins, which include ancient walls, bastions, and a beautiful temple.

The fort is less crowded compared to other tourist spots, making it an ideal location for a peaceful day trip.

How to Reach

By Train

The nearest railway station is Tarapur, which is well-connected to Mumbai. Local trains run frequently.

Distance from station to fort: 5 km

By Road

Take NH48 from Mumbai towards Gujarat. The drive takes about 2.5 hours. Buses and taxis are also available.

Distance from Mumbai: 120 km

Local Transport

Auto-rickshaws and taxis are available from Tarapur station to the fort. Renting a bike is also an option for local exploration.

Tips & Guidelines

Do's

Don'ts

  • Don't litter around the fort
  • Avoid climbing on unstable structures
  • Don't disturb wildlife
  • Don't visit during heavy rains
